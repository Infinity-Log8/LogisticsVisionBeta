# 30-60-90 Day Plan

The plan for standing up an AI-native team from zero. Phased so you build the
team in the order that compounds — quality bar first, then production capacity.

## Days 1-30: Foundation

**Goal:** Hire the two anchor roles. Set up tooling. Define the first product milestone.

**Hires (in order):**
1. **Tech Lead / Architect** (target: signed offer by day 25)
   - Why first: sets engineering quality bar and AI-pairing culture
   - Sourcing: warm intros + targeted outbound, not job-board postings
   - Bar: senior staff/principal-level operator who has shipped at scale
2. **Strategic Marketer** (target: signed offer by day 30)
   - Why second: positioning has to be settled before there's content to ship
   - Sourcing: industry network, founders who've been positioned well by someone

**Founder/CEO work (parallel):**
- Set up tier 1 + tier 3 tooling stack (see `/tooling/stack.md`)
- Stand up the prompt library and knowledge base
- Define the first product milestone — what we're building, for whom, why now
- Draft the first ICP and positioning hypothesis (the strategic marketer will sharpen
  it when they start)
- Establish the weekly rhythm from day one, even with a team of three

**Deliverables by day 30:**
- 2 hires signed, start dates within 30 days
- Foundation tooling stack operational
- First product milestone defined (one page)
- Hiring pipeline for the next 4 roles (designer, 2 engineers, content marketer)

## Days 31-60: Production Capacity

**Goal:** Ship the first feature through the AI-augmented lifecycle. Build the
content and design foundation.

**Hires (in order):**
3. **Senior Product Designer** (target: signed by day 45)
   - Sets design system from scratch — every day of delay compounds
4. **Mid-Senior Engineer #1** (target: signed by day 50)
5. **Mid-Senior Engineer #2** (target: signed by day 60)

**Team work:**
- Tech lead defines the engineering quality bar, code review norms, and CI/CD setup
- Strategic marketer publishes a positioning manifesto and builds the messaging stack
- Designer starts the design system (one component for every two real usages)
- First feature moves through the full feature lifecycle (discovery → ship)
- Daily async standup + Monday cross-functional sync established

**Deliverables by day 60:**
- 5 hires signed (4 of them started)
- First feature shipped to production end-to-end
- Positioning manifesto published (internally + external blog post)
- Design system v0 with 8-12 core components
- First retro completed — at least one workflow adjusted

## Days 61-90: Refine and Scale

**Goal:** Fill remaining roles. Run a full feature lifecycle as a complete team.
Establish quality gates based on real output.

**Hires (in order):**
6. **Content / Growth Marketer** (target: signed by day 70)
7. **Visual / Brand Designer** (target: signed by day 80, can be later if budget tight)
8. **DevOps / Infrastructure Engineer** (target: signed by day 85)
9. **Product Manager** (target: signed by day 90)

**Team work:**
- First full feature lifecycle with all functions represented
- First quality audit (Friday of week 10)
- First ROI dashboard populated with real numbers
- Risk register updated with anything observed in the first 90 days
- All workflows reviewed and adjusted based on real friction

**Deliverables by day 90:**
- 8-9 hires signed (target full team within 30 more days)
- Two features shipped end-to-end
- 8-12 long-form content pieces published
- First quarterly ROI review completed with real data
- Workflows finalized for v1; quality gates calibrated to actual output

## Optional Phase 2 (Days 91-180)
- UX Researcher (contract or fractional)
- 3rd Mid-Senior Engineer (if scope demands)
- Data / Performance Marketer (or fractional contractor)

## Hiring Operating Principles
- **No "B" hires.** A B-level hire on an AI-native team becomes a quality drift vector.
- **Move fast on yes, fast on no.** A weak signal in week 2 of interviews is a no.
- **Reference checks are mandatory and structured.** Same questions every time.
- **Founder/CEO in every final-round conversation** until the tech lead is hired,
  then tech lead pairs in for engineering hires.
