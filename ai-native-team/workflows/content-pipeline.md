# Content Pipeline

How AI-augmented content gets produced without sounding like AI-augmented content.
Five stages, each with a clear handoff and quality gate.

## 1. Ideation
**Owner:** Content/Growth Marketer (with Strategic Marketer input)
**AI does:** Cluster topic ideas from customer support tickets, sales call notes,
search query data, competitor content gaps, and trending industry conversation.
Generates a topic backlog with target keywords, suggested format (post, thread,
video, doc), and rough audience fit score.
**Human does:** Picks what to actually write. Adds the spike — the point of view
or surprising claim that makes a piece worth reading. AI can't pick the spike.
**Output:** Weekly topic plan with 3-5 pieces, each with a one-sentence angle.

## 2. Drafting
**Owner:** Content/Growth Marketer
**AI does:** Generate a first draft from a tight brief (audience, angle, key claims,
desired length, tone). Generates 5-10 headline options. Drafts intros, transitions,
and conclusions. Suggests evidence and examples.
**Human does:** Rewrites for voice. Adds specifics, opinions, and real examples.
Cuts the generic AI-isms ("In today's fast-paced world..."). Verifies every claim.
If the AI draft is 70% there, ship the edit. If it's 30% there, throw it out and
start over — don't polish slop.
**Output:** A draft that reads like a human wrote it. Because a human did.

## 3. Review
**Owner:** Strategic Marketer (brand) + subject expert (accuracy)
**AI does:** Check against brand voice guide — flag tone mismatches. Check for
factual claims that need a source. Suggest a clearer rewrite for any flagged section.
**Human does:** Read it. Ask whether it would persuade a skeptical reader. Push
back on any claim that's too convenient. Final editorial approval.
**Quality gate:** No content ships without strategic marketer or designated
editor approval. No exceptions.
**Output:** Approved-to-publish piece.

## 4. Distribution
**Owner:** Content/Growth Marketer (+ Data/Performance Marketer if exists)
**AI does:** Reformat the piece for each channel (blog → tweet thread → LinkedIn
post → newsletter → video script). Suggest optimal posting times by audience.
Generate alt text and meta descriptions.
**Human does:** Confirms the reformatted versions actually work in their native
format — not just compressed copies of the original. Hits publish.
**Output:** Multi-channel distribution. One piece, five surfaces.

## 5. Analysis
**Owner:** Content/Growth Marketer (+ Data/Performance Marketer)
**AI does:** Weekly performance report — what performed, what didn't, what patterns
across pieces. Surface anomalies worth investigating.
**Human does:** Asks what changed and why. Decides which threads to pull harder
on next cycle. Updates the topic backlog with what's working.
**Output:** A two-paragraph weekly write-up. Feeds back into Ideation for next week.

## Volume Targets
A 2-person marketing team operating this pipeline can sustainably produce:
- 2-4 long-form pieces per week (blog/essays/docs)
- 10-20 short-form pieces per week (threads, posts, emails)
- 1-2 launch campaigns per month
- Without burning out, because AI does the production weight

The human bottleneck is editing and judgment, not typing.
