# Weekly Rhythm

The team's operating cadence. Designed so AI does the prep, summarization, and
status-tracking that traditional teams burn meetings on — and humans use the
freed time for decisions and deep work.

## Monday — Cross-Functional Sync (30 min)
**Who:** Everyone
**Format:** AI pre-generates a packet by 9am Monday including:
- Last week's shipped work (commits, designs, content) auto-summarized
- Blockers surfaced from async standups
- Top 5 customer feedback themes from the past week
- Metrics movement worth noting (good or bad)

**In the room:** Team reads the packet for the first 5 minutes silently, then spends
25 minutes on decisions only — no status updates. If something isn't a decision,
it goes back to async.

## Daily — Async Standup
**Who:** Everyone
**Format:** Each person posts 3 lines in the team channel by 10am:
- What I shipped yesterday
- What I'm working on today
- Where I'm stuck (if anywhere)

**AI summarizer** posts a digest at 10:30am: who's blocked on what, who could unblock
whom, any pattern across the team. The digest is one short paragraph, not a wall.

## Mid-Week — Deep Work Blocks
**Tuesday-Thursday:** No internal meetings between 9am-12pm and 2pm-5pm except
critical incident response. This is when actual product, design, and content
get built. Calendar should look mostly empty.

Function-specific syncs (eng review, design crit, content review) happen in the
1-2pm slot when needed — never daily, only when there's something to review.

## Friday — Lightweight Retro (30 min)
**Who:** Everyone
**Format:** AI prepares a "week in review" packet:
- Commit and shipping velocity vs. last few weeks
- Design iterations and approvals
- Content output and early engagement signal
- Customer feedback themes
- Anything that broke or almost broke

**In the room:** What worked, what didn't, what to change next week. One concrete
change agreed to per retro. No more.

## Weekly Office Hours
- Tuesday 4-5pm: Tech lead office hours (design and marketing can drop in)
- Thursday 4-5pm: Strategic marketer office hours (eng and design can drop in)

Optional. Often empty. That's fine.

## What This Replaces
- Daily standups in a meeting room
- Weekly status reports nobody reads
- Wednesday "alignment" meetings
- Quarterly planning theater that produces a deck and no action

## Why It Works
The packets do the boring work. Humans show up to argue about real tradeoffs. The
asymmetry between "AI summarizes" and "humans decide" is the productivity unlock.
