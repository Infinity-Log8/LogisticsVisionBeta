# Senior Architect / Tech Lead

**Reports to:** Founder / CEO
**Function:** Engineering
**Note:** First engineering hire. Sets quality bar and AI-pairing culture for the whole org.

## Core Responsibilities
- Own system architecture: data model, service boundaries, key abstractions
- Lead code review and uphold the engineering quality bar
- Make build/buy and language/framework decisions; document trade-offs
- Mentor engineers without becoming a bottleneck
- Spend ~40% of time coding on the highest-leverage problems

## AI-Augmented Responsibilities
- **AI handles:** generating ADRs from a decision brief, exploring multiple
  architectural approaches in parallel, drafting RFC documents, summarizing long
  threads, writing first-pass migration plans, generating test scaffolding
- **Human judges:** which approach is actually right for this codebase and team,
  when AI's suggestion is plausible-but-subtly-wrong, when to challenge AI's default
  conventions, where to invest in long-term abstractions vs. ship-now duct tape

## Key Skills to Hire For
- Has architected at least one system in production at non-trivial scale
- Strong code review instinct — can read 200 lines of diff and find the one real issue
- Writes clearly — RFCs, ADRs, post-mortems
- Comfortable across at least two language ecosystems
- Treats AI as a power tool — uses it heavily, trusts it carefully, reviews everything

## Interview Signals (AI-native vs. traditional)
- **Strong:** Can describe an AI-generated suggestion they rejected and why
- **Strong:** Has shipped a non-trivial feature recently and can walk through the
  diff line-by-line, including which parts were AI-pair-programmed
- **Strong:** Has opinions on test strategy, observability, and rollout safety, not
  just frameworks
- **Strong:** Has run an incident as the IC — can describe their decisions, not just
  the timeline
- **Weak:** Frames AI as "the future" or "a fad" — either is a tell
- **Weak:** Can't describe a recent technical decision they're embarrassed about
  (everyone has one; pretending otherwise is the red flag)

## Compensation Band (US, mid-range senior IC / staff)
- Base: $220K - $280K
- Equity: 0.5% - 1.5% (early-stage) — this hire is force-multiplier-grade
- Total comp target: $320K - $450K
