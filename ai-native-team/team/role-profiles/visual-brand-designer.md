# Visual / Brand Designer

**Reports to:** Founder / CEO (works closely with Senior Product Designer and Marketing)
**Function:** Design

## Core Responsibilities
- Own brand identity: logo system, type system, color, photography direction, motion
- Produce campaign and content assets (web, social, email, decks, swag)
- Maintain the brand guideline document and asset library
- Set and enforce visual consistency across product, marketing, and internal docs
- Partner with Marketing on launch assets and Engineering on motion implementation

## AI-Augmented Responsibilities
- **AI handles:** generating image variations, exploring illustration directions,
  removing backgrounds, upscaling, drafting motion descriptions, producing first-pass
  social cuts in multiple aspect ratios
- **Human judges:** what fits the brand voice, when a generation looks generic or
  off-brand, when to commission a human illustrator instead of generating, how to
  evolve the brand without breaking recognition

## Key Skills to Hire For
- Distinctive taste — can articulate what makes a brand feel specific, not generic
- Cross-medium fluency: print, screen, motion, environmental
- Strong relationship with type, color, and grid
- Editorial restraint — knows when to subtract
- Comfortable with Figma, motion tools (Rive, After Effects, Lottie), and at least
  one generative tool (Midjourney, Firefly, DALL-E, Runway)

## Interview Signals (AI-native vs. traditional)
- **Strong:** Has used AI to explore 50 options for a brand mark and can show why the
  chosen one is the answer — judgment is visible in the edit
- **Strong:** Portfolio includes work that obviously can't be AI-generated (custom
  illustration, hand-set type) alongside AI-augmented production work
- **Strong:** Can describe a moment where AI output looked "fine" and they rejected it
- **Weak:** Beautiful but homogeneous portfolio — every brand looks like every other
  Y Combinator brand
- **Weak:** Refuses to discuss AI tools, or uses them with no point of view

## Compensation Band (US, mid-range senior IC)
- Base: $150K - $180K
- Equity: 0.15% - 0.4% (early-stage)
- Total comp target: $190K - $240K
