# Product Manager

**Reports to:** Founder / CEO
**Function:** Cross-functional (sits across Design, Engineering, Marketing)

## Core Responsibilities
- Own the product roadmap and prioritization
- Write specs, decision briefs, and launch plans
- Run feature discovery: synthesize research, customer signal, and strategy into bets
- Coordinate cross-functional work — keep Design, Eng, and Marketing in lockstep
- Own product metrics: usage, retention, NPS, and feature-level KPIs

## AI-Augmented Responsibilities
- **AI handles:** drafting specs from a discovery brief, summarizing customer
  interviews, generating multiple roadmap framings for the same set of inputs, drafting
  meeting notes and decision summaries, producing a first-pass FAQ from a spec
- **Human judges:** which bet to make, when a spec is too detailed vs. not detailed
  enough, what trade-off the team should actually argue about, who needs to be in the
  room for a decision

## Key Skills to Hire For
- Has shipped products with measurable customer impact
- Writes plainly — specs read like memos, not bingo cards
- Strong cross-functional instinct — knows what Design needs vs. what Eng needs vs.
  what Marketing needs from a spec
- Comfortable holding ambiguity — doesn't force premature precision
- Treats AI as a draft generator, not a decision maker

## Interview Signals (AI-native vs. traditional)
- **Strong:** Brings a spec they wrote that includes the tradeoffs considered and the
  one they rejected with rationale
- **Strong:** Can describe a feature they killed or didn't ship and why
- **Strong:** Has used AI to synthesize customer feedback and can point to the part
  that required human reading
- **Weak:** Confuses output (specs written, meetings held) with outcome (products
  shipped, metrics moved)
- **Weak:** Defers all decisions to "the data" — abdicates judgment
