# Budget Model

Comparison: traditional 20-25 person org vs. AI-native 8-12 person team. The
headcount savings are the obvious part. The speed advantage is the real win.

## Traditional Team (20-25 people)

Fully-loaded annual cost (US, mid-market, includes benefits, taxes, equipment,
office, ~1.3x salary multiplier):

| Function | Headcount | Avg. fully-loaded | Subtotal |
|----------|-----------|-------------------|----------|
| Design (Sr. Designer, Brand, Researcher, Design Lead) | 4-5 | $230K | $1.0M |
| Engineering (Tech Lead, 6-8 engineers, 1-2 DevOps) | 9-11 | $260K | $2.5M |
| Marketing (Lead, Content, Growth, Designer, Analyst, PMM) | 5-6 | $200K | $1.1M |
| PM / Cross-functional (PM, Producer, Project Mgr) | 2-3 | $230K | $0.6M |
| Tooling (per-seat licenses, traditional stack) | — | $5K/person | $0.1M |
| **Annual total** | **20-25** | | **~$3.2M - $4.2M** |

## AI-Native Team (8-12 people)

| Function | Headcount | Avg. fully-loaded | Subtotal |
|----------|-----------|-------------------|----------|
| Design (Sr. Designer, Brand, fractional Researcher) | 2-3 | $260K | $0.6M |
| Engineering (Tech Lead, 2-3 engineers, DevOps) | 3-5 | $310K | $1.3M |
| Marketing (Strategic, Content, optional Data) | 2-3 | $250K | $0.6M |
| PM (cross-functional) | 1 | $300K | $0.3M |
| Tooling (AI stack, foundation models, connective layer) | — | $700/mo per person | $0.08M |
| **Annual total** | **8-12** | | **~$2.0M - $2.5M** |

## Net Comparison

| Metric | Traditional | AI-Native | Delta |
|--------|-------------|-----------|-------|
| Headcount | 20-25 | 8-12 | ~55% fewer people |
| Annual cost | $3.2M - $4.2M | $2.0M - $2.5M | ~35% lower |
| Hiring time to full team | 12-18 months | 6-9 months | ~50% faster |
| Tooling spend | $100K | $80-100K | Roughly equal |

**Annual savings:** $1.2M - $1.7M. Over 3 years, that's $3.6M - $5.1M.

## The Speed Advantage (Bigger Than Cost)

The real ROI isn't the headcount line — it's cycle time. Smaller teams with shared
context move faster. Specifically:

- **Fewer coordination points:** 10 people have 45 possible 1:1 pairs.
  25 people have 300. Communication overhead grows quadratically with headcount.
- **Shared context:** Everyone knows what everyone is working on. No re-explaining
  to a "PM proxy" or "tech writer translator."
- **Faster feedback loops:** Customer signal → roadmap → ship cycle compresses from
  6-8 weeks to 2-3 weeks because the synthesis and drafting layer is automated.
- **Lower cost of experimentation:** Trying a new positioning, new feature, new
  channel is cheap. You can run 3-4 experiments in the time a traditional team
  runs 1.

## What the Savings Don't Capture
- Equity dilution: 12 people share equity vs. 25, which matters at exit
- Hiring fatigue: a smaller team has less recruiting overhead, less onboarding tax
- Decision velocity: fewer people means fewer approvals and fewer "let's loop in X"
- Real estate: 10 people fit in a quarter of the office space of 25

## Where to Spend the Savings
The AI-native bet pays best when reinvested. Suggested allocation of the ~$1.5M
annual savings:
- ~40% in higher per-person comp to attract the editing/judgment talent the model
  needs ($600K to top up senior bands)
- ~30% in tooling and infrastructure ($450K — better dev environment, better data
  warehouse, better AI tooling as the frontier moves)
- ~30% on the bottom line / runway extension ($450K)

A team that pockets the savings and underpays its IC bar tends to fail. A team
that reinvests in compounding leverage tends to compound.
