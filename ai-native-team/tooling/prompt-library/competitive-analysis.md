# Competitive Analysis Prompt

Use to analyze a competitor's positioning, features, and likely strategy. The output
informs positioning, roadmap, and content responses.

## When to Use
- A competitor ships a major feature or releases a redesign
- Pricing changes in the category
- New competitor enters the space
- Quarterly competitive review

## The Prompt

```
You are a senior competitive intelligence analyst. Analyze [competitor name] using
the materials below. Be honest about what they do well, not just what's vulnerable.

Source materials:
- Website: [URL]
- Pricing page: [URL]
- Recent product announcements: [URLs or paste content]
- Public customer reviews from G2/Capterra/Reddit: [paste 10-20 reviews if available]
- Their recent thought-leadership content: [URLs]

Context about us:
- Our positioning: [one paragraph]
- Our target audience: [who we sell to and why they choose us]
- Where we overlap with this competitor: [feature areas, customer segments]

Produce the analysis in these sections:

1. Positioning summary
   - How they describe themselves in their own words
   - What category they're playing in
   - The promise/value they lead with

2. Target audience signal
   - Who they're really for, based on the content and case studies
   - Where this differs from their stated audience

3. Feature surface
   - Top 5-7 features they emphasize
   - What's clearly mature vs. clearly thin
   - Anything missing that customers expect in the category

4. Pricing and packaging
   - Their model (freemium, seat-based, usage-based, etc.)
   - Implicit segmentation revealed by tier breaks
   - How this compares to us

5. Customer sentiment
   - Most common praise themes
   - Most common complaint themes
   - Specific quotes worth remembering

6. Strategic read
   - What bet are they making about the market?
   - Where are they likely investing next?
   - What are they NOT doing that they probably should?

7. Implications for us
   - Where we should sharpen our positioning to differentiate
   - Where we should not chase them (and why)
   - Content/marketing responses worth considering

Be specific. Avoid generic competitive-analysis language. If a finding could apply
to any competitor in any category, it's not useful — cut it.
```

## How to Use Output
- AI is good at synthesis and pattern recognition across the source materials.
- AI is bad at strategic intent inference — section 6 is the most speculative.
- Always verify pricing and feature claims against the source — AI hallucinates here.
- The "implications for us" section should be the strategic marketer's editing, not
  AI's output.
