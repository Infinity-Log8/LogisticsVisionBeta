# Content Brief Prompt

Use to convert a product spec, feature ship, or strategy doc into a content brief
that the content marketer can draft from.

## When to Use
- Feature is two weeks from launch and needs a launch post
- Strategy has shifted and the website/positioning needs to reflect it
- Customer feedback theme is worth a long-form essay
- Competitor moved and we need a response piece

## The Prompt

```
You are a senior content strategist. Generate a content brief from the source material
below. The brief should be detailed enough that a content marketer can draft from it
without re-reading the source.

Source material:
[paste spec, ship notes, customer quotes, strategy doc, etc.]

Audience context:
- Primary audience: [e.g. "logistics ops directors at mid-market shippers"]
- What they care about: [their measurable goals]
- Where they encounter our content: [blog, LinkedIn, email, newsletter]

Brand voice:
- [3-5 adjectives describing how we write — e.g. "direct, specific, opinionated, plainspoken"]
- Avoid: [list things our brand doesn't do — e.g. "buzzwords, hedging language, faux-humility"]

Produce a brief with these sections:

1. Working title (offer 3 options, ranked)
2. The angle in one sentence — the spike, the surprising or specific claim
3. Who this is for and what they should do after reading
4. Key points to make, in priority order (5-7 bullets)
5. Evidence/examples to include (with sources if known)
6. Structural shape (e.g., "problem → why existing solutions fall short → our approach → proof → CTA")
7. Target length and format
8. Distribution plan (primary channel + 2-3 derivatives)
9. SEO keywords if relevant
10. What this piece is NOT (helps the writer avoid scope creep)

End with: a one-paragraph "why this matters now" rationale the writer can adapt
for the opening.
```

## How to Use Output
- Treat the brief as a starting point; the writer's editing makes it good.
- AI is reliable on structure, audience analysis, and key points.
- AI is weak on the "spike" — the writer often needs to find a better angle than the
  one AI suggests.
- If the AI brief reads generic, the source material was probably too thin. Add more.
